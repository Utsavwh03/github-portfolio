At a glance

Hi, I’m Utsav Dhanuka, an MS Computer Science student at UMass Amherst and a B.Tech graduate in CSE from IIT Kharagpur. I’m a graduate researcher at the BioNLP Lab, where I develop agentic reinforcement learning systems and multimodal reasoning models that enable AI to perceive, plan, and act in complex, real-world settings.

Previously, at Veda Quant AI, I built multi-agent trading frameworks that applied reinforcement learning and adaptive decision-making to financial markets—work that deepened my interest in the intersection of intelligent agents, reasoning, and real-world optimization.

My current research explores how reinforcement learning, process supervision, and visual grounding can be combined to enhance interactive multimodal agents capable of tool use, curiosity-driven exploration, and compositional reasoning.

Broadly, I’m passionate about creating safer, interpretable, and autonomous AI systems that bridge the gap between language, perception, and action.
